Research

I am an aspiring applied microeconomist whose primary interests consist of the use of alternative data (e.g., remote sensing, text, images) and applications of deep learning and machine learning methods in Economics. I am interested in deriving novel economic insights from the use of multi-modal data as well as complementing the more conventional, traditional methods used in the field. Currently, I am a first-year PhD student in Economics at Vancouver School of Economics (VSE), University of British Columbia (UBC). I completed the MS in Economics and Computational Science (MSEC) program at Duke University in 2024.

Prior to these, I worked as a Senior Research Associate at the Environment and Climate Change sub-cluster, Economic Cluster at BRAC Institute of Governance and Development (BIGD), BRAC University. I started my career at Innovations for Poverty Action, Bangladesh (IPA-B) as an Associate Research Analyst in 2019.
